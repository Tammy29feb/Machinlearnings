Decision Trees:
The If-Then Path

A decision tree is a flowchart-like structure in which each internal node represents a "test" on an attribute. It's the most intuitive way machines make choices.

Maximum Information Gain

The algorithm chooses splits that reduce entropy (uncertainty) the most at each step.

Overfitting Prevention

Pruning techniques ensure the tree doesn't grow too deep and memorize noise instead of patterns.

Recursive Partitioning Visualization

Finding the Hyperplane.

Support Vector Machines (SVM) find the ultimate boundary that separates different data universes with maximum breathing room.

Large Margin

Unlike other models that just separate points, SVM maximizes the distance to the nearest data points (support vectors).

The Kernel Trick

When data cannot be separated by a straight line, SVM projects it into a higher dimension where a linear cut becomes possible.

Robustness

By focusing only on support vectors, SVM remains effective even in high-dimensional spaces with few samples.
